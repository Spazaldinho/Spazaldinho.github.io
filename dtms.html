Perception Development Researcher - Queen’s AutoDrive

  • Working on the Perception Development Team to design advanced computer vision algorithms for an autonomous vehicle.
  • Researching and implementing machine-learning algorithms for detection and classification systems.

Queen's AutoDrive is a dynamic and skilled team of engineers and computer scientists, committed to designing an SAE Level-4 autonomous vehicle by 2025, as one of the exclusive Canadian participants in the prestigious GM-SAE AutoDrive Challenge II. This project not only symbolizes a leap towards autonomous vehicular technology but also embodies the team's dedication to enhancing vehicular safety and efficiency through advanced computer vision algorithms and machine learning techniques.

Electrical Design Team Member - Queen’s Hyperloop

  • Developing electronic systems for power and controls onboard an autonomous tunnel boring machine.
  • Participated in the Canadian Hyperloop Conference and the European Hyperloop Conference.

Queen's Hyperloop is at the forefront of pioneering rapid transit technology, utilizing ultra-low-pressure tubes to potentially enable passenger pods to travel at speeds exceeding 700 km/hr, significantly reducing travel time from Montreal to Toronto to under 39 minutes with zero direct carbon emissions. In addition, the team is actively engaged in the Not-a-Boring Competition, aiming to bring a functional Tunnel Boring Machine (TBM) by 2025, showcasing their dedication to innovative and sustainable transportation solutions.
